Life Insurance Myths Debunked: What Canadians Need to Know

May 28, 2023 Malik Bachlani

Life insurance is one of the most important tools for protecting your family’s financial future. Yet, many Canadians hesitate to get coverage due to persistent myths and misconceptions. In this article, we’ll debunk some of the most common life insurance myths so you can make informed decisions for your loved ones.

Myth #1: Life Insurance Is Too Expensive

Many people overestimate the cost of life insurance. In reality, term life insurance can be very affordable, especially if you’re young and healthy. The sooner you get coverage, the lower your premiums are likely to be.

Tip: Even a small policy can make a big difference for your family in a time of need.

Myth #2: I Don’t Need Life Insurance If I’m Single or Young

Life insurance isn’t just for parents or homeowners. If you have debts, co-signers, or want to lock in low rates while you’re young, life insurance can be a smart move. It can also help cover funeral costs and leave a legacy for loved ones or charities.

Myth #3: My Work Coverage Is Enough

Employer-provided life insurance is a great benefit, but it’s often not enough to fully protect your family. Group policies may not be portable if you change jobs, and coverage amounts are usually limited.

  • Review your workplace policy and consider supplementing it with personal coverage.
  • Personal policies stay with you, no matter where you work.

Myth #4: Life Insurance Is Only for Death Benefits

Some life insurance policies, like whole life or universal life, can build cash value over time. This can be accessed for emergencies, education, or retirement planning. Life insurance can be a flexible part of your overall financial strategy.

Myth #5: It’s Too Complicated to Get Life Insurance

With the help of a licensed advisor, the process can be straightforward. Many applications can be completed online or over the phone, and your advisor will guide you through every step.

Remember: The right policy depends on your unique needs and goals. A professional can help you find the best fit.

How to Get Started

Don’t let myths hold you back from protecting your family. Speak with a licensed advisor to review your options and get a personalized recommendation.

  • Assess your current and future financial needs.
  • Compare different types of policies and coverage amounts.
  • Ask questions and make sure you understand your options.
