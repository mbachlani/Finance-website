Registered Disability Savings Plan (RDSP)

The RDSP is a powerful government-supported savings plan to help Canadians with disabilities and their families build long-term financial security.

The Registered Disability Savings Plan (RDSP) is one of the most powerful yet underutilized financial tools available in Canada. Nearly one million eligible Canadians haven’t opened an RDSP, missing out on thousands of dollars in free government contributions and the chance to build lifelong financial security for themselves or their loved ones with disabilities.

What is an RDSP?

The RDSP is a government-registered savings plan designed to help individuals with disabilities and their families save for the future. Anyone who qualifies for the Disability Tax Credit (DTC) can open one. The money inside grows tax-free, and when withdrawn, only government contributions and investment earnings are taxed in the beneficiary’s name—often at a very low rate.[3]

How the Government Helps

The Government of Canada directly contributes to RDSPs through two key programs:

  • Canada Disability Savings Grant (CDSG): The government matches annual contributions by up to 300%, depending on family income. A lifetime total of up to $70,000 in grants is available.[4][5]
  • Canada Disability Savings Bond (CDSB): For low-income Canadians (under about $37,500), the government deposits up to $1,000 each year without requiring any contributions—up to $20,000 total.[2][5]

These contributions make the RDSP one of the most generous savings vehicles available anywhere. Even families with limited means can grow tens of thousands of dollars in savings over time—often entirely from government deposits.

Short-Term Benefits

  • Immediate financial boost: Government grants and bonds often multiply initial savings right away, meaning families see instant growth from their contributions.[4]
  • Tax-deferred growth: Investments inside the RDSP grow without being taxed until money is withdrawn.[1]
  • No impact on disability benefits: RDSP savings don’t reduce eligibility for provincial or federal disability assistance programs, including Ontario Disability Support Program (ODSP), the Canada Child Benefit, or GST credits.[6]

Long-Term Benefits

  • Secure future income: RDSPs are designed for lifelong financial planning, creating a source of income in adulthood or retirement for those living with disabilities.[7]
  • Massive compounding potential: With grants, bonds, and investment growth, an RDSP can easily grow to hundreds of thousands of dollars over a few decades.[8][6]
  • Flexible withdrawals: Money can be used for any purpose—medical expenses, education, housing, or personal needs—giving beneficiaries true financial independence.[4]

What Millions Are Missing

Despite the incredible advantages, many Canadians either don’t know about the RDSP or are unsure how to start. One study estimates around 1.5 million Canadians are eligible, yet only a fraction have opened accounts. That’s billions of dollars in untapped grants and bonds sitting unclaimed.[1]

Final Thoughts

The RDSP isn’t just a savings account—it’s a lifeline. It’s how the Government of Canada ensures people with disabilities can look forward to a financially secure, dignified future. For families, it’s an opportunity to make a long-term impact with even small contributions.
